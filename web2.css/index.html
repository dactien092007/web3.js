HẠ LONG BAY

Hạ Long Bay is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Quảng Ninh Province, Vietnam. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, featuring thousands of limestone karsts and islets that rise dramatically from the emerald waters of the Gulf of Tonkin. The bay is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the world who come to explore its unique landscape, take boat tours, and enjoy activities such as kayaking and swimming. Hạ Long Bay is also home to diverse marine life and has significant cultural and historical importance in Vietnam.
